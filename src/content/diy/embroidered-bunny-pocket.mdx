<div class="sqs-html-content">
	<p>
		I decided to embroider a little bunny to sit in the pocket of my apron. It's
		perfect for Easter and just general adorableness. It's always nice to have
		something fun to make you smile. 
	</p>
	<p>
		I made a template so you can add one to your own apron, pocket tee or
		jacket! Whatever pocket you want. And if you've never embroidered before,
		don't worry, there's just two simple stitches to learn. 
	</p>
	<p>
		WHAT YOU'LL NEED
		<br />
		<a target="_blank" href="/s/Pocket-Bunny-Template.pdf">
			Bunny Pocket Template

		<a
			target="_blank"

		>
			pattern tracing paper
		</a>
		<br />
		pencil, <em>I used a white colored pencil</em>
		<br />
		<a
			target="_blank"
			href="https://www.amazon.com/Clover-Gold-Embroidery-Needles-Pack/dp/B000YQEVYQ/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1489576761&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=embroidery+needle"
		>
			embroidery needle
		</a>
		<br />
		<a
			target="_blank"
			href="https://www.amazon.com/s/ref=sr_pg_3?fst=p90x%3A1%2Cas%3Aon&amp;rh=n%3A2617941011%2Ck%3Aembroidery+thread&amp;page=3&amp;keywords=embroidery+thread&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1489576810&amp;spIA=B00FYBDWCS,B00USHJNC6,B01N7CDZNE,B01FDKIUB2,B0035DBUPS,B01AWAJ4R2"
		>
			embroidery thread
		</a>
		, <em>I went with white to be subtle against my grey apron</em>
		<br />
		scissors
	</p>

<div class="sqs-html-content">
	<p>
		First take your template and pattern tracing paper and trim them down to the
		length of the pocket edge line on the template. Note that you can scale the
		template when printing to make it bigger or smaller depending on the size of
		your pocket. 
	</p>
</div>
<div
	class="image-block-outer-wrapper layout-caption-below design-layout-inline"

<div class="sqs-html-content">
	<p>
		With the template over the pattern paper, align the line to your pocket and
		trace the template with your pencil. The paws should go on the pocket
		itself. Make sure you press hard enough to really get the pattern on your
		fabric. 
	</p>
</div>
<div
	class="image-block-outer-wrapper layout-caption-hidden design-layout-inline"

<div class="sqs-html-content">
	<p>
		Thread your needle with the embroidery thread and start embroidering your
		bunny head on using a backstitch. Here is a{' '}
		<a
			target="_blank"
			href="http://www.dmc-usa.com/Education/How-To/Learn-the-Stitches/Embroidery-Stitches.aspx"
		>
			guide to embroidery stitches
		</a>
		. Make sure you start a little lower than pocket for your bunny head to make
		sure if you put something in the pocket, or it moves, people can't see the
		start of it. 
	</p>
</div>

	class="image-block-outer-wrapper layout-caption-hidden design-layout-inline"

<div class="sqs-html-content">
	<p>
		After you've backstitched the outline of the head and the nose, backstitch
		the paws onto the pocket. Then use french knots to make the two eyes. Here's
		a{' '}
		<a target="_blank" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W1Cq24d5-8s">
			video on the french knot
		</a>
		. It can be pretty tricky so you may have to cut them out and redo them a
		couple times. 
	</p>
	<p>And there you have it! </p>
