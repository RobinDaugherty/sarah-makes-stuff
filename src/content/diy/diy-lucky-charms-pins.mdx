{<p>Preheat oven to 325 degrees F.Â Trace the Lucky Charms template onto the matte
	shrink film with a black Sharpie. Then color in with the paint markers.</p>}
{<p><Picture
		src={ContentImage2}
		alt="DIY Lucky Charms Pins | Sarah Makes Stuff"
	/></p>}
{<p>Cut out the charms and place on a cookie sheet lined with parchment paper.
	Place in preheated oven for 3-4 minutes. Make sure you watch the whole time
	and take out once the pieces have flattened. They'll start warping at first,
	don't worry, that's what they're supposed to do.</p>}
{<p>Once they're done shrinking, take out of the oven and let completely cool.
	Once cooled, brush on a light layer of glaze and let dry 24 hours.</p>}
{<p><Picture
		src={ContentImage3}
		alt="DIY Lucky Charms Pins | Sarah Makes Stuff"
	/></p>}
{<p>Once the glaze has dried completely. Take pin backs and super glue and glue on
	the backs. Let completely dry and there you go!</p>}
{<p><Picture
