		I desperately needed a desk in my guest room/office. I mean, it's not much

		+ fancy leg route. I'm not up to doing actual woodworking at this point. My
		father has lost 2.5 fingers due to circular saws so I stay far, far away
		from those. I promise it would take something crazy to loose a finger making
		the desk I came up with.
	</p>
	<p>
		I went with the{' '}
		<a
			target="_blank"
			href="http://www.ikea.com/us/en/catalog/products/80278287/"
		>
			HILVER table top from Ikea
		</a>
		, but you could probably use any table top. It is made out of bamboo and I
		thought it would look pretty sweet with a some hairpin legs. I'm pretty much
		obsessed with hairpin legs at this point guys. 
	</p>

<div class="sqs-html-content">
	<p>Assembling was actually a lot easier than I thought it would be!</p>
	<p>
		<strong>WHAT YOU'LL NEED</strong>
		<br />
		<a
			target="_blank"
			href="http://www.ikea.com/us/en/catalog/products/80278287/"
		>
			Ikea HILVER table top
		</a>
		<br />4{' '}
		<a
			target="_blank"
			href="https://www.hairpinlegs.com/products/hairpin-leg?variant=325861935"
		>
			28" hairpin legs
		</a>
		<br />
		12 1" screws, make sure your screw are shorter than your chosen table top! 
		<a
			target="_blank"
			href="http://www.ikea.com/us/en/catalog/products/00169249/"
		>
			<em>(I used the short screws from this IKEA set)</em>
		</a>
		<br />
		Drill and drill bit that is thinner than your screws
	</p>
	<p>
		First you have to measure out where your holes are going. I did this by
		lining up the legs so they we're just inside the corner two predrilled holes
		(you can kind of see this better in the next pic). I then took a pencil and
		marked where the holes in the table leg were. Then I just drilled into the
		center of each of those spots, making sure that I didn't drill through the
		desk! I noticed with this table top that the corner holes were harder to
		drill through, it's apparently solid all the way through, and the two outer
		holes for each corner of the desk are in an air pocket area. So don't be
		alarmed when your drill suddenly flies down during those. 
	</p>
