{<p><strong>LONDON FOG BLONDIE INGREDIENTS</strong> <br />1 cup unsalted butter,
melted
<br />2 cups dark brown sugar
<br />2 large eggs
<br />
1/2 cup brewed earl grey tea
<br />1 tbsp vanilla extract
<br />2 cups all-purpose flour
<br />
1/8 tsp salt
<br />
<br />
<strong>LONDON FOG FROSTING INGREDIENTS</strong>
<br />8 ounces cream cheese, room temperature
<br />
1/2 cup unsalted butter, room temperature
<br />
1/4 cup heavy whipping cream
<br />3 bags of earl grey tea
<br />1 tsp vanilla extract
<br />
3-3.5 cups powdered sugar</p>}
{<p><strong>LONDON FOG BLONDIE INSTRUCTIONS</strong>
<br />
<span>
Heat oven to 350º F. Line a 9x13-inch metal baking pan with parchment.
</span></p>}
{<p>Using an electric mixer, beat together melted butter and brown sugar until
blended. Add eggs, tea, and vanilla. Continue beating until smooth.</p>}
{<p>In a separate bowl, whisk together salt and flour. Add dry ingredients to wet
ingredients and beat until just incorporated.</p>}
{<p>Spread the batter evenly into prepared pan and bake 20 to 25 minutes, until a
toothpick inserted into the middle comes out clean. Set aside to cool.</p>}
{<p>Once cooled, frost with London Fog Frosting (recipe below) and cut into
squares.</p>}

{<p><strong>LONDON FOG FROSTING INSTRUCTIONS</strong>
<br />
Heat ¼ cup of heavy cream in microwave for 30 seconds. Add tea bags to cream
and steep for 15 minutes. Remove tea bags and squeeze out tea. Place in the
fridge to chill.</p>}
{<p>Using an electric mixer, beat butter and cream cheese together on medium speed
until light and fluffy, about 3 minutes. Add tea-infused heavy cream and
vanilla and mix together for another minute.</p>}
{<p>With mixer running, add powdered sugar in ½ cup increments. Add more or less
powdered sugar depending on the consistency you like. Mix until incorporated.
