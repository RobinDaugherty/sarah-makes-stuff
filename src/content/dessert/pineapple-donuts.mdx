<div class="sqs-html-content">
	<p>
		<strong>
			DONUT INGREDIENTS
			<br /> 
		</strong>
		<span>140 grams (1 cup) all-purpose flour</span>
		<br />
		<span>100 grams (1/2 cup) granulated sugar</span>
		<br />
		<span>1 tbsp baking powder</span>
		<br />
		<span>1 egg</span>
		<br />
		<span>115 grams (1/2 cup) milk</span>
		<br />
		<span>1/2 tsp vanilla</span>
		<br />
		<span>4 tbsp vegetable oil</span>
		<br />
		<span>6 ounces green candy melts</span>
	</p>
	<p>
		<span>
			<strong>DONUT ICING INGREDIENTS</strong>
			<br />1 cup powdered sugar
			<br />2 tbsp milk
			<br />
			1/2 tsp vanilla extract
			<br />4 drops of liquid food coloring
		</span>
		<br /> 
	</p>
	<p>
		<span>
			<strong>DONUT INSTRUCTIONS</strong>
		</span>
		<br />
		<span>
			Sift dry ingredients together. Add egg, vanilla, and milk and beat 1
			minute with electric mixer.
		</span>
	</p>
	<p>Add oil and continue to beat 1 minute more.</p>
	<p>
		<em>If using a donut maker,</em> fill and bake donuts according to your
		donut maker. 
		<a
			target="_blank"
			href="https://www.amazon.com/Holiday-Time-Mini-Donut-Maker/dp/B008FCF7W6"
		>
			This is the donut maker I have
		</a>
		. It was a cheap, impulse buy around Christmas a couple years ago. I'm SURE
		there are better ones out there. 
	</p>
	<p>
		<em>If using a donut baking tin,</em> spoon the batter into baking tin.
		Place in oven preheated to 350 F oven and bake for 8-12 minutes, or until a
		toothpick inserted into donut comes out clean. Take out of the oven and
		allow to cool in the pan for 5 minutes before taking out onto a wire rack to
		cool completely.
	</p>
	<p>Make icing (recipe below). </p>
	<p>
		Dip donuts into icing. Place on cooling rack with parchment paper underneath
		to catch the icing that drips and let dry. Dot with black frosting. Set
		aside.
	</p>
	<p>
		Melt candy melts per instructions. With a piping bag fitted with a No. 1
		round decorating tip, pipe out stems onto parchment paper. I made five
		leaves with a thick stalk at the bottom. Make enough for the amount of
		donuts you have. Let dry completely.
	</p>
	<p>
		Once dry, with a paring knife, make a slit in each donut just big enough for
		the stems and insert stems.
	</p>
	<p>
		<strong>DONUT ICING INSTRUCTIONS</strong>
		<br />
		In<strong> </strong>a small bowl, mix sugar, milk, and vanilla until smooth
		and the sugar has completely dissolved. 
	</p>
	<p>Add in food coloring and continue mixing until the color is uniform. </p>
	<p>
		Quickly dip donuts before it starts to dry out. If it does dry out, just add
		a little more milk until it's the right consistency.
	</p>
</div>
